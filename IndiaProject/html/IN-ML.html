Famous Places

Double Decker Living Root Bridge

Umshiang Double-Decker Root Bridge is a living root bridge in Meghalaya, India. It can be reached via trekking 45 kilometres (28 mi), or 6500 steps, from Cherrapunji. There is also a waterfall nearby.

Dawki River

The Dawki River, also known as the Umngot River, is a river in the state of Meghalaya, India. It's a popular tourist attraction known for its clear waters and boat rides.

Krang Suri Falls

Krang Suri Falls can be found in Jaintia Hills. It is one of Meghalaya's most beautiful and picturesque waterfalls. Lush greenery surrounds this magnificent streaming of water and glistening blue liquid.

Nakham Bitchi

Nakham Bitchi is made of dry fish with vegetables and is a famous non-veg food of Meghalaya. This traditional soup features sun-dried or fire-dried fish, simmered with vegetables to create a rich, thick broth.

About

Meghalaya is a state in northeast India. Meghalaya was formed on 21 January 1972 by carving out two districts from the state of Assam: the United Khasi Hills and Jaintia Hills and the Garo Hills. The estimated population of Meghalaya in 2014 was 3,211,474. Meghalaya covers an area of approximately 22,429 square kilometres, with a length-to-breadth ratio of about 3:1.The state is bound to the south by the Bangladeshi divisions of Mymensingh and Sylhet, to the west by the Bangladeshi division of Rangpur, and to the north and east by India's State of Assam. The capital of Meghalaya is Shillong. During the British rule of India, the British authorities nicknamed it the "Scotland of the East".English is the official language of Meghalaya. Unlike many Indian states, Meghalaya has historically followed a matrilineal system where the lineage and inheritance are traced through women; the youngest daughter inherits all wealth and she also takes care of her parents.
