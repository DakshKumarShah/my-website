Famous Places

Chitrakote Waterfall

The Chitrakote Falls is a natural waterfall on the Indravati River, located approximately 38 kilometres to the west of Jagdalpur, in Bastar district in the Indian state of Chhattisgarh. The height of the falls is about 29 metres. It is the widest waterfall in India, reaching a width of nearly 300 metres during the peak monsoon season. Because of its width and horseshoe shape during the monsoon season, it is often called the "Niagara Falls of India".

Ibrahim Rauza

The Ibrahim Rauza is a funerary complex featuring a mausoleum and a mosque, which are very similar in style, with a fountain between them. Following the example of many Indo-Islamic monuments, the complex is raised on a plinth within an enclosed gated space. It was built in 1627 by Taj Sultana, the wife of Sultan Ibrahim Adil Shah II. It is located in Bijapur. The construction process reflects the magnificence of the building since it took more than eight years to finish. It is by far one of the most remarkable artistic achievements of the Adil Shahi dynasty, who established a Sunni Sultanate in Bijapur in the 15th century and ruled for almost two hundred years.

Ghatarani Waterfalls

Riverside spot for bathing around a forest waterfall, with a Hindu temple, shops & food stalls.

Achanakmar Wildlife Sanctuary

Achanakmar Wildlife Sanctuary is a sanctuary in Mungeli district of Chhattisgarh and in the Anuppur and Dindori districts of Madhya Pradesh in India. It was established in 1975, under the provisions of the Indian Wildlife Protection Act of 1972, and declared as a Tiger Reserve under Project Tiger, in 2009.It is a part of the Achanakmar-Amarkantak Biosphere Reserve.

Famous Food

Muthia

Steamed dumplings made from rice batter, seasoned with spices, and garnished with sesame seeds and coriander leaves. Muthia is a popular breakfast dish in Chhattisgarh.

Bafauri

A protein-rich, steamed snack made from soaked and ground chana dal, mixed with spices, and served with chutney or yogurt.

Khurma

A sweet dish made from wheat and rice flour.

Chilla

A popular breakfast dish in Chhattisgarh.

Tradition

Chhattisgarh is renowned for its rich artistic traditions and handicrafts. The state is known for its unique metal craft, bell metal work, terracotta art, and wood carvings. Bastar Dhokra, a traditional form of metal casting, is highly regarded for its intricate designs.

About

Chhattisgarh is a landlocked state in Central India. It is the ninth largest state by area, and with a population of roughly 30 million, the seventeenth most populous. It borders seven states (Uttar Pradesh, Madhya Pradesh, Maharashtra, Jharkhand, Odisha, Andhra Pradesh and Telangana. Formerly a part of Madhya Pradesh, it was granted statehood on 1 November 2000 with Raipur as the designated state capital.Chhattisgarh is one of the fastest-developing states in India. Its Gross State Domestic Product is ₹5.09 lakh crore (2023–24 est.), with a per capita GSDP of ₹152,348 (2023–24 est.). A resource-rich state, it has the third largest coal reserves in the country and provides electricity, coal, and steel to the rest of the nation. It also has the third largest forest cover in the country after Madhya Pradesh and Arunachal Pradesh with over 40% of the state covered by forests.
